What is cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from digital attacks. These attacks can aim to access, change, or destroy sensitive information, extort money from users, or disrupt normal business operations. In essence, cybersecurity involves measures and technologies designed to safeguard electronic data from unauthorized use or criminal activities3. This includes protecting against threats like malware, phishing scams, data breaches, and ransomware.

What is cyberattack?

A cyberattack occurs when confidential data of an individual or a company is exposed to an unauthorized outsider. For instance, someone you don’t know might obtain your bank card information and secret codes, or access your data by connecting to your social networks like Facebook or Twitter (X). Another example is when someone acquires the methods and necessary information that a company uses to pay its employees, and with great skill, manages to access your phone or computer, allowing them to control it remotely without any of your permission. This is what a cyberattack is, and the person behind it is called a hacker.
